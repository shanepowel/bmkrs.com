# bmkrs voice

The thing that stops us sounding like everyone else. Read this before writing any copy for the site.

---

## 1. Where the voice comes from

Most brand agencies are creatives who picked up some business sense. We are the other way round. We came from the delivery floor: building teams and shipping hard things inside big, regulated organisations. We kept watching brilliant products lose to worse ones that were simply better told. So we pointed the rigour we already had at brand.

That origin is the voice. We talk like builders, not like an awards-show stage. We are precise because we have been accountable for things that had to actually work. We are plain because fluff is what you reach for when you have nothing to show. And we are a little blunt because we have earned the right to be.

**The one-line positioning:** a brand company run by builders.

**The thesis:** the better-told brand wins. we make sure it is yours.

---

## 2. How we sound

Five principles. Each is a test you can run on a sentence.

1. **Concrete over abstract.** Name the real thing. Not "we elevate brands," but "we name it, build the look, write the words, and ship the site." If a sentence would survive being moved to a competitor's site, it is too abstract. Rewrite it.

2. **Say it once, properly.** One true line beats three clever ones. Cut the second adjective. Cut the warm-up sentence. Get to it.

3. **Show the trade-off.** Honesty is the differentiator. We say what something costs, what we would not do, when an idea is not working. Copy that only ever sells reads like every other agency. Copy that admits something reads like a person.

4. **Dry, not zany.** There is room for wit, but it is deadpan, never wacky. A good line is usually just the truth said flatly.

5. **Plain English, lowercase, British.** No em dashes. Short sentences. Real words. If a clever client would not say it out loud in a meeting, do not write it.

## 3. Words and phrases we never use

These are the tells of category sameness. Banned on the site, no exceptions.

- bold, boldly, unmistakable
- passionate, we live and breathe, obsessed with
- bespoke solutions, tailored solutions
- storytelling, storytellers, narrative journey, the journey
- elevate, unlock, supercharge, amplify, ignite
- world-class, best-in-class, cutting-edge, next-level, game-changing
- disrupt, disruptive, move the needle, secret sauce, magic
- "we're not just an agency"
- "turn heads," "stand out from the crowd," "make an impact" (unqualified)
- data-driven, results-driven (say the actual result instead)
- DNA (as in "brand DNA"), synergy, holistic
- "thought leadership" (just call them articles, or notes)

If you catch one, the fix is almost always to replace it with the specific thing it was standing in for.

## 5. The "and" device

Our whole argument is a rejection of either/or. Companies buy brand from one agency, voice from another, pr from a third, product from a fourth, then spend the year translating between them. We are the "and": all of it, one team.

Use the conjunction deliberately as a structural device, especially in headlines and the "what we do" section:

> brand. and voice. and pr. and the product behind it. one team, not five.

It also plays off the name. b-makers. we make the whole thing.

---

## 6. Messaging hierarchy

Three lengths of the same idea. Use the one that fits the space.

**One line (hero, meta, social bio):**
the better-told brand wins. we make sure it is yours.

**Short (about intro, link previews):**
a brand company run by builders. we make the brand, find the voice, get it heard, and build the product behind it. all from one team, since 2013.

**Long (about, pitch openers):**
we started inside big, complicated organisations, building the teams and delivery that made ambitious things ship. we kept seeing good products lose to worse ones that were simply better told. so we built the other half: the brand, the voice, the noise that makes people care, with the same rigour we used to ship software. more than a decade on, that is the whole job.
