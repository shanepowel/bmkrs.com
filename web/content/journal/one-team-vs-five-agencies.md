# one team vs five agencies: the real cost of handoffs

here is how most brands get built. a branding agency does the identity and leaves. a web agency interprets the pdf they left behind. a copywriter freelances the words around the design. a pr agency, hired later, invents its own version of the story. a marketing agency runs ads written by none of the above. and in the middle: you, the client, working as an unpaid integration layer between five suppliers who have never met.

every one of those handoffs costs something. mostly it costs the thing you were buying in the first place.

## what gets lost in translation

strategy degrades at each handoff like a photocopy of a photocopy. the positioning had nuance; the brand book captured most of it; the web agency read the summary; the ad agency read the website. by the fifth supplier, the brand is operating on rumours of the original idea. nobody is incompetent. everyone is downstream.

then there is the contradiction tax. the website says one thing, the press release another, the ads a third, each internally fine, collectively incoherent. customers experience all of them as one brand, and one brand that disagrees with itself reads as untrustworthy without anyone being able to say why.

and the slowest cost: accountability dissolves. when results disappoint, the web agency blames the brand, the marketing agency blames the website, and the diagnosis meeting has five defendants and no verdict.

## the coordination work is real work

founders rarely budget for what the five-agency model demands of them: briefing the same context five times, refereeing disagreements between suppliers, noticing the drift, and chasing the gaps that every supplier assumed was someone else's scope. that is a part-time job, performed by the most expensive person in the company.

## what one team changes

when the same team does the strategy, identity, voice, website, pr and growth, the handoffs simply do not exist. the person who wrote the positioning is in the room when the homepage hero gets designed. the launch story comes from the people who built the thing being launched. nothing falls between the gaps because there are no gaps, and when something underperforms, there is exactly one team responsible for fixing it.
