# website copywriting that converts: clarity is the whole game

when a website underperforms, everyone blames the design. the redesign ships, the numbers barely move, and the actual culprit survives untouched: the words. visitors do not leave because your gradient is wrong. they leave because eight seconds in, they cannot answer the only question they arrived with: what is this, and is it for me?

website copywriting is not decoration on top of design. it is the part doing the selling.

## the hero has one job

your hero section must answer what you do, who for, and why you, in the time it takes to decide whether to scroll. that rules out the entire genre of poetic heroes: "reimagine what is possible", "where ideas come to life", "empowering tomorrow". these are the copywriting equivalent of a receptionist refusing to say what the company does.

the test is brutal and useful: show a stranger your hero for five seconds, take it away, and ask them what the company sells. if they cannot say, nothing below the fold gets a chance to work.

## structure is copy

conversion copywriting is mostly deciding what comes in what order. the sequence that works for almost everyone: the claim, then the proof, then how it works, then who it is for, then what to do next. objections answered where they occur, not corralled into an faq graveyard. one call to action repeated, not five competing ones. most "weak copy" is decent sentences in a broken order.

## write like a person, cut like an editor

the voice should sound like your brand, but conversion adds a discipline on top: every sentence must move the reader towards the next one. read the page aloud; everywhere you stumble, a visitor bounces. cut the adjectives that assert quality and replace them with the evidence that proves it. specific beats grand, every time it is tested.
