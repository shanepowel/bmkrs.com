# seo for new brands: rank for what you sell, not what you blog

the default startup seo strategy is a content treadmill: publish twice a week, cover the whole industry, wait for traffic. a year later there are ninety posts, a few hundred visits, and no pipeline, because the traffic that came was never going to buy anything. the posts ranked for what was easy, not for what the company sells.

new brands do not have a content volume problem. they have a focus problem.

## topical authority beats topical breadth

search engines increasingly reward sites that demonstrably know one subject deeply over sites that touch many subjects lightly. for a new domain with no history, this is the entire opening: pick the narrow territory that maps to what you sell, and own it completely before writing a word about anything else.

practically, that means defining one core topic, the problem you solve, and building a cluster around it: one substantial page on the topic itself, supported by eight to fifteen pieces answering the specific questions buyers ask on the way to purchasing. each piece links to its siblings and up to the core page. that structure tells both the reader and the crawler the same thing: this is what we are about.

## write for the buying journey, not the news cycle

the highest-value keywords for a new brand are rarely the biggest ones. "how to choose a [your category]", "[your category] pricing", "[competitor] alternatives", "[problem] vs [adjacent solution]": these are searched by people with budgets, in the act of deciding. they are also exactly the pages most startups never write, because they feel less glamorous than commentary. write them first. commentary can come once the commercial layer ranks.

## the brand is a ranking factor now

branded search, people googling your name, is one of the strongest signals a new domain can send, and you cannot content-calendar your way to it. it comes from pr, from a founder with a point of view, from a product people mention. this is why we treat seo as one instrument in the growth section rather than a solo act: the [press launch](/services#press-launch) that earns links and the positioning that makes you memorable are doing seo work too, whether or not anyone calls it that.

## the honest timeline

a focused cluster on a new domain starts moving in three to six months and compounds from there. anyone promising page one in weeks is selling you something that will not survive the next core update.
